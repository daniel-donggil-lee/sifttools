## Use Cases: Where the New Pricing Delivers

Before getting into features and pricing, the practical question: what workflows does Writesonic handle well, and where does it fall short?

### Blog Content at Volume
Writesonic's core strength is blog production. The AI Article Writer accepts a target keyword, optional outline, and tone selection, then generates a 1,200-2,500 word draft with structured H2/H3 headings, an introduction, and a conclusion. The output follows standard blog architecture rather than producing a wall of unformatted text.

For teams publishing regularly, the Lite plan includes 15 articles/month, Standard bumps to 30, and Growth offers 50. The article-based allocation replaces the old word-count model, making it easier to predict monthly output.

### Product Descriptions
The Product Description template generates 150-200 word descriptions from basic product specs in about 8 seconds. The output follows e-commerce copywriting conventions (benefit-led opening, feature list, call to action) without requiring the user to specify a framework.

### Content Refresh and Repurposing
ChatSonic, the conversational interface, handles content transformation well: converting a blog post into a LinkedIn article, summarizing a white paper into an email sequence, or rewriting existing copy for a different audience. Because ChatSonic pulls current web data, references to recent events and trending topics stay accurate.

### Where It Falls Short
Technical content, detailed product comparisons, and anything requiring domain expertise produces mediocre results. The AI generates plausible-sounding text that frequently contains inaccuracies when covering specialized topics. This is an industry-wide AI limitation, but it is worth noting because Writesonic's speed and volume can create a false sense of confidence in output quality.

## AI Article Writer: The Feature That Sells the Tool

The AI Article Writer is Writesonic's flagship content generator and the primary reason to choose it over cheaper alternatives like ChatGPT for blog production.

The workflow: input a target keyword, select article length (500-3,000 words), choose a tone (professional, casual, witty, etc.), and optionally provide an outline. The tool generates a complete draft in 30-60 seconds, including an SEO-aware title, meta description, introduction, body sections with subheadings, and a conclusion.

Compared to Jasper's Boss Mode, the Article Writer requires less manual direction. Boss Mode operates as a collaborative editor — the user steers each section with specific commands. The Article Writer aims for a complete first draft from a single input. The trade-off: Boss Mode produces more controlled output; the Article Writer produces faster output that may need more structural editing.

Compared to Copy.ai's chat-based long-form workflow, the Article Writer generates better-structured articles. Copy.ai's chat interface can produce long-form content, but the output often lacks clear heading hierarchy and logical section progression.

All plans now include built-in content optimization and SEO auditing tools — a significant addition that the old pricing lacked. The Lite plan includes 6 audits/month (200 pages), scaling up to 50 audits/month (2,500 pages) on the Growth plan.

## ChatSonic: Conversational AI with Web Access

ChatSonic differentiates itself from Jasper's chat and Copy.ai's chat with one feature: real-time web access. While competitors rely on training data with knowledge cutoffs, ChatSonic pulls current information from Google search results when generating content.

This matters for content about recent events, trending topics, new product launches, or rapidly changing industries. A blog post about "2026 email marketing trends" written through ChatSonic will reference current data rather than generating plausible-sounding but outdated claims.

The accuracy rate for web-sourced claims is not perfect — any AI tool that synthesizes search results can misinterpret or incorrectly attribute information. Fact-checking remains essential, but the starting point is closer to current reality than tools working from static training data.

ChatSonic's responses are included with all plans alongside the Article Writer allocation. The AI agent generations are unlimited on the Standard plan and above, making conversational iteration more practical than under the old word-count model.

## Bulk Generation: The Agency Feature

CSV import allows uploading 50+ topics, keywords, or product specs in a single batch. Writesonic processes them sequentially, generating one draft per row. This feature is included on the Pro plan — Jasper charges extra for comparable bulk capabilities.

For agencies managing content calendars across multiple clients, bulk generation turns a day of manual article creation into an hour of review and editing. The workflow: prepare a spreadsheet of topics and keywords, upload to Writesonic, generate drafts, then review and edit each piece before publishing.

The limitation: bulk-generated content tends toward uniformity. Articles on similar topics will share structural patterns, transitional phrases, and argumentation styles. Human editing is essential not just for accuracy but for differentiation — publishing 15 articles that read identically will not serve a content strategy regardless of how efficiently they were produced.

## What's Missing

**Plagiarism detection**: Not included. Generated content must be verified through external tools before publishing. At $49/mo+, this omission is notable given that Writesonic now competes at a higher price tier than before.

**Brand Voice customization**: Limited to preset tone categories. No ability to train the AI on existing brand content. This is the single biggest feature gap compared to Jasper and Copy.ai, and the primary reason teams prioritizing brand consistency may choose an alternative tool.

**Image quality**: Writesonic includes AI image generation, but the output quality lags significantly behind Midjourney, DALL-E 3, or even Canva's AI tools. The images are adequate for placeholder purposes but not publication-ready for most professional contexts.

## Bottom Line

Writesonic's value proposition has evolved from "most words per dollar" to "most complete SEO writing platform at a mid-range price." The AI Article Writer produces structured, usable blog drafts, and the built-in content optimization and SEO auditing tools eliminate the need for a separate Surfer SEO subscription for many teams.

The $49/mo Lite plan is no longer the budget champion it once was, but the integrated SEO tooling adds genuine value that competitors charge separately for. For blog-first content operations that need both AI writing and SEO optimization in one platform, Writesonic remains a strong choice in 2026.
