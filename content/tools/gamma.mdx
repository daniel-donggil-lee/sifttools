## What is Gamma? (And Why I Switched to It)

I discovered Gamma after spending 4 hours creating a client pitch deck in Canva, only to realize I'd wasted half that time hunting for the right stock photos and wrestling with alignment. Gamma is an AI-powered presentation tool that generates complete slide decks from text prompts, then lets you refine the content and design without starting from a blank canvas.

Unlike Canva or Beautiful.AI, Gamma doesn't just offer templates — it actually writes your content and selects relevant visuals based on your topic. After 3 months of testing, I can create a 20-slide investor presentation in the time it used to take me to pick a template.

## What I Actually Use It For

My main use case is client strategy presentations. I'll input something like "Create a social media strategy presentation for a B2B SaaS company targeting marketing directors" and Gamma generates slides covering market analysis, content pillars, KPIs, and implementation timeline. The AI nails about 70% of the content, then I spend 15-20 minutes customizing specifics.

I also use it for internal team updates. Last month I needed to present Q3 marketing results to our leadership team. Instead of copying data into PowerPoint slides manually, I fed Gamma our key metrics and let it structure the narrative flow. The result looked more polished than anything I would've built from scratch.

The third workflow that surprised me: educational content. When I needed to create a 12-slide explainer on SEO fundamentals for new team members, Gamma organized the concepts logically and included relevant examples I hadn't thought of.

## The Features That Matter

### Smart Grid Layout System
This is Gamma's secret weapon for making presentations look professional without design skills. The Smart Grid automatically balances text blocks, images, and white space based on your content length. I've tested this with everything from text-heavy strategy slides to image-focused product showcases, and it consistently produces cleaner layouts than Canva's auto-resize feature.

### AI Content Generation
The content quality impressed me more than expected. Gamma doesn't just create bullet points — it writes full sentences and suggests logical slide progressions. When I generated a presentation about "email marketing best practices," it included specific tactics like A/B testing subject lines and segmentation strategies, not generic fluff. The AI seems trained on actual business content, not just marketing copy.

### One-Click Export Options
Gamma exports to PowerPoint (.pptx), PDF, and shareable web links without the formatting disasters I've experienced with Google Slides or Canva. I tested a 25-slide presentation across all three formats — fonts, spacing, and image positioning stayed consistent. The web link option is particularly useful for client reviews since they can comment directly without downloading files.

### What's Missing
I wish Gamma had slide animation capabilities beyond basic transitions. If you need complex animations or interactive elements, you'll still need PowerPoint or Keynote. The template library is also smaller than Canva's — about 200 presentation themes versus Canva's thousands.

The Pro plan delivers solid value compared to Canva Pro ($15/month) or Beautiful.AI ($12/month), especially considering the AI content generation saves hours of writing time. The free plan's 3-generation limit means you'll hit the wall quickly if you're serious about using it.

## Who Should (and Shouldn't) Use Gamma

**Good fit if you:**
- Create 5+ presentations per month for clients or internal teams
- Struggle with design skills but need professional-looking slides
- Want to speed up the initial content creation process

**Skip it if you:**
- Need heavy animation features or interactive presentations
- Work primarily offline without internet access
- Already have a design team creating custom slide templates

## Final Verdict

After 3 months with Gamma, it's become my go-to tool for presentation first drafts and simple client deliverables. The AI content generation genuinely saves time, and the Smart Grid system produces cleaner designs than I could create manually in Canva or PowerPoint.

I'd recommend the Pro plan at $10/month for anyone creating multiple presentations monthly. Start with a business strategy or educational topic to test the AI's content quality — if it nails 70% of your slides on the first try, you'll save enough time to justify the cost. Skip it if you need advanced animations or work primarily offline.
